  1. Mona Lisa

    The Mona Lisa is a 16th-century oil painting by Leonardo da Vinci, considered the world's most famous and recognized artwork. It depicts Lisa Gherardini, an Italian noblewoman, in a revolutionary, forward-facing pose, renowned for its subject's enigmatic smile and masterful sfumato technique. After being acquired by the king of France, the painting has been housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris, where its global fame surged after being stolen in 1911 and recovered two years later.

    The Subject

    • The portrait is widely believed to be Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a Florentine silk merchant, Francesco del Giocondo.
    • Her Italian name, "La Gioconda," is derived from her husband's surname.

    The Artist

    • Leonardo da Vinci, a master of the Italian Renaissance, created the painting between 1503 and 1519.
    • He may have continued working on it until shortly before his death.
